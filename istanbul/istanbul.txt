<h2 style="color:white;">HISTORY AND IMPORTANCE: </h2>
<p style="color:white;">Istanbul is the largest city in Turkey and the country's economic, cultural and historic center. The city straddles the <br>
Bosphorus strait, and lies in both Europe and Asia, with a population of over 15 million residents, comprising 19% of the <br>
population of Turkey. Istanbul is the most populous city in Europe, and the world's fifteenth-largest city.<br>
Founded as Byzantion by Megarian colonists in 657 BCE, and renamed by Constantine the Great first as New Rome (Nova Roma) <br>
during the official dedication of the city as the new Roman capital in 330 CE, which he soon afterwards changed as <br>
Constantinople (Constantinopolis), the city grew in size and influence, becoming a beacon of the Silk Road and one <br>
of the most important cities in history. It served as an imperial capital for almost sixteen centuries, during the <br>
Roman/Byzantine (330–1204), Latin (1204–1261), Byzantine (1261–1453), and Ottoman (1453–1922) empires. It was <br>
instrumental in the advancement of Christianity during Roman and Byzantine times, before its transformation to an <br>
Islamic stronghold following the Fall of Constantinople in 1453 CE. In 1923, after the Turkish War of Independence, <br>
Ankara replaced the city as the capital of the newly formed Republic of Turkey. In 1930 the city's name was officially <br>
changed to Istanbul, an appellation Greek speakers used since the eleventh century to colloquially refer to the city.</p>
